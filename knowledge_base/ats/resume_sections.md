# Resume Sections Guide

This guide explains the purpose of each major resume section and the information it should contain. A well-structured resume makes it easier for both recruiters and Applicant Tracking Systems (ATS) to understand a candidate's qualifications.

---

# Contact Information

## Purpose

Provide recruiters with accurate information to contact you.

## Include

- Full name
- Phone number
- Professional email address
- City and country (or city and state)
- LinkedIn profile
- GitHub profile (if relevant)
- Portfolio or personal website (if applicable)

## Avoid

- Photos (unless specifically requested)
- Full mailing address
- Personal information such as age, gender, marital status, or nationality unless required.

---

# Professional Summary

## Purpose

Introduce yourself in two to four sentences.

## Include

- Current role or education
- Primary area of expertise
- Key technical skills
- Career objective

## Best Practices

- Tailor it for each application.
- Mention your strongest qualifications.
- Keep it concise.

---

# Work Experience

## Purpose

Show professional experience and demonstrate measurable impact.

## Include

For every position:

- Job title
- Company
- Location
- Employment dates
- Bullet points describing achievements

## Best Practices

- Use reverse chronological order.
- Begin each bullet with an action verb.
- Quantify achievements whenever possible.
- Focus on outcomes instead of responsibilities.

Example:

- Reduced API response time by 35%.
- Automated reporting, saving 10 hours per week.

---

# Projects

## Purpose

Demonstrate practical experience, especially for students and early-career professionals.

## Include

For every project:

- Project name
- Technologies used
- Brief description
- Individual contribution
- Measurable results (if available)
- GitHub or live demo link (if available)

## Best Practices

Prioritize projects that are relevant to the target role.

---

# Skills

## Purpose

Provide recruiters and ATS with a clear overview of your technical abilities.

## Include

Examples:

- Programming languages
- Frameworks
- Libraries
- Databases
- Cloud platforms
- Development tools
- Version control
- Operating systems

## Best Practices

- Group similar skills together.
- List only skills you can demonstrate.
- Prioritize skills mentioned in the job description.

---

# Education

## Purpose

Present academic qualifications.

## Include

- Degree
- Institution
- Graduation year
- GPA (optional)
- Relevant coursework (optional)
- Honors and awards (optional)

Recent graduates may place Education before Work Experience.

---

# Certifications

## Purpose

Highlight additional professional qualifications.

## Include

- Certification name
- Issuing organization
- Completion date

Only include certifications relevant to the target role.

---

# Achievements

## Purpose

Highlight accomplishments beyond work experience.

Examples include:

- Scholarships
- Competitions
- Hackathons
- Research publications
- Awards
- Open-source contributions

Use measurable achievements whenever possible.

---

# Volunteer Experience

## Purpose

Demonstrate leadership, teamwork, or community involvement.

## Include

- Organization
- Role
- Dates
- Key contributions

Volunteer work can strengthen resumes with limited professional experience.

---

# Languages

## Purpose

Highlight language proficiency when relevant.

Example:

- English — Fluent
- Spanish — Intermediate
- German — Basic

---

# Interests

## Purpose

Provide additional information about personal interests when appropriate.

Examples:

- Robotics
- Competitive programming
- Photography
- Chess
- Marathon running

Only include interests that add value or demonstrate transferable skills.

---

# References

References are generally not required.

Instead of writing:

"References available upon request."

omit the section unless the employer explicitly requests references.

---

# Recommended Resume Order

For most applicants:

1. Contact Information
2. Professional Summary
3. Work Experience
4. Projects
5. Skills
6. Education
7. Certifications
8. Achievements
9. Volunteer Experience
10. Languages
11. Interests

Students and recent graduates may place Education above Work Experience.

---

# Resume Section Checklist

A complete resume should include:

- Contact information
- Professional summary
- Work experience
- Skills
- Education

Optional sections include:

- Projects
- Certifications
- Achievements
- Volunteer experience
- Languages
- Interests

Include optional sections only when they strengthen the application.
